Class Cash Book

A complete guide to recording class dues, tracking expenses, and reporting the class cash position — for treasurers, homeroom teachers, and anyone helping manage it.

1Introduction

Class Cash Book is a single web app for running your class's cash (kas kelas) from end to end: recording each student's monthly dues, printing receipts for parents, logging what the money was spent on, and always knowing the real bank balance.

It works two ways:

  • Connected to Firebase — your data is saved online and synced in real time. This is what lets you share a public report link, add other users, and access your data from any device.
  • Local mode — if there's no internet connection or Firebase isn't set up, the app still works and saves data in your browser only (good for a quick trial, but data won't sync across devices).

The sidebar on the left is your main menu. Everything below is grouped the same way it's organized there: money coming in first, then money going out, then the bank position and account access, then setup.

2Logging In

When Firebase is connected, you'll see a login screen first.

  1. Enter the email and password given to you by your class admin/treasurer.
  2. Click Log In.
  3. Forgot your password? Enter your email in the field, then click Forgot password? Send a reset link to the email above.
No account yet? This app doesn't have public sign-up. Ask your class admin to add your email under Manage Users first.

If you log in successfully but see a message that your account has no access yet, it means your email was authenticated but hasn't been added to this class's user list — ask the admin to add you.

3School Years

Every school year is its own separate "book." Students, payments, accounts, activities, and expenses for one school year are never mixed with another — but old years are never deleted either; you can switch back and view them anytime.

Switching years: use the School Year dropdown at the top of the sidebar. Whatever you pick there applies to every tab in the app until you change it again.

To create a new school year, click + New School Year in the sidebar, or go to Settings → Manage School Years. You can optionally copy the student list from a previous year — only the student roster copies over, payment history always starts fresh at zero for a new year.

4Dashboard

Your at-a-glance summary for the currently selected school year. It shows:

CardWhat it means
Total StudentsHow many students are on the roster this school year
Paid This MonthHow many students have paid for the actual current calendar month
Collected This MonthTotal amount collected for the current month
Total Cash This School YearEverything collected so far this school year
Total Expenses This School YearEverything spent so far this school year
Bank Balance (Bank Mandiri)The real running balance — see Cash Balance

Payment Status table

Below the stat cards is a filterable list of every student's paid/unpaid status:

  • Month — pick any month within the school year period, not just the current one.
  • Status — All / Unpaid only / Paid only.
  • Search — filter by student name.
Tip: switch Status to Unpaid only to instantly get your "who still needs to pay" list for any month.

5Student Records

This is your class roster for the selected school year.

  1. Click + Add Student.
  2. Fill in roll number, full name, parent/guardian name, and phone/WhatsApp number (all optional except name).
  3. Special Dues Amount is optional — leave it blank if the student pays the same default amount as everyone else (set in Settings). Only fill it in if this particular student's dues are different.
  4. Click Save.

Use the search box to find a student by name. Click the ✎ icon to edit, or 🗑 to delete.

Note: a student can't be deleted if they already have payment history — delete their payment records first (in Payment Entry's history table) if you really need to remove them.

6Payment Entry

This is where you record a student paying their monthly dues.

  1. Select the student from the dropdown.
  2. Click each month the student is paying for — a green/highlighted chip means already paid and can't be selected again; click to select one or several months in a single transaction (e.g. paying 3 months at once).
  3. Set the payment date, method (Cash, Transfer, or QRIS), and an optional note.
  4. Check the Total Amount preview, then click Save Payment.
Tip: months are shown by their full name now (not abbreviated) so there's no ambiguity, and the payment period always runs July → June regardless of calendar year.

Payment History

Below the form is a searchable list of every payment recorded this school year. From here you can:

  • ✎ Edit — change the months, amount, date, method, or note on an existing payment.
  • 🗑 Delete — removes the payment; the related month(s) go back to unpaid.
  • 🖨 Print Receipt — jumps straight to the Print Receipt tab for that transaction.

7Print Receipt

Find any payment transaction here (search by student name) and generate a proof-of-payment receipt for parents.

ButtonWhat it does
🖨 PrintOpens the browser's print dialog with a nicely formatted receipt
🖼️ ImageDownloads the receipt as a PNG image, ready to send via chat
📄 PDFDownloads the receipt as a PDF file
💬 WAOpens WhatsApp with a pre-written message to the parent's saved phone number (requires the student to have a phone number on file)

The receipt includes the school and class name, student and parent name, months paid, period, amount, payment method and date, a receipt number, and signature lines for the parent and the class treasurer. If a student has paid in full for the entire school year, a "PAID · 1 YEAR · CLASS" stamp appears automatically.

8Report Summary

The full-class view of who has paid for which months — a 12-column matrix (one column per month of the school year) with every student as a row.

  • Total Collected, Payment Completeness (% of all possible student-months paid), and Paid Slots are shown as summary stats above the table.
  • Click 🖨 Print Report to print the whole matrix — it automatically switches the page to landscape (since the table is wide) and switches back afterward.

9Sharing With Parents (Public Link)

At the bottom of the Report Summary tab is a card called Share With Parents (Public Link). This creates a link anyone can open — no login required — showing a safe, read-only snapshot of the class cash position.

Privacy by design: the public link never includes phone numbers or parent names. It only shows student names, monthly paid/unpaid status, total collected, and the bank balance.
  1. Click Publish / Update Public Link.
  2. Copy the link that appears and share it in your class WhatsApp group or similar.
Important: this is not live/real-time. The link only shows data as of the last time you clicked Publish. After recording new payments, click Publish / Update Public Link again whenever you want parents to see the latest numbers.

Requires Firebase this feature needs an online connection — it won't work in local mode, since there's nowhere shared to publish the link to.

10Chart of Accounts

Found in Settings. This is the list of expense categories your Expense Ledger report will be grouped by — for example 6900-00-011 — Class Supplies, matching the account-code style used in formal bookkeeping software.

  1. Click + Add Account.
  2. Enter an account code (e.g. 6900-00-012) and a name (e.g. "Market Day 2025").
  3. Click Save.
An account can't be deleted while any Activity is still linked to it, and each account code must be unique within the school year.

11Activities

Also in Settings, right below Chart of Accounts. Activities are what you actually pick when logging an expense — each one is mapped to one account above.

  1. Click + Add Activity (you need at least one account created first).
  2. Name the activity (e.g. "Daily Class Needs", "Market Day 2025") and choose its linked account.
  3. Click Save.

Setting a default activity

Click the ⭐ icon next to whichever activity you use most often (e.g. "Daily Class Needs"). It gets a Default badge and will be pre-selected automatically every time you open Expense Entry — no more picking it from the dropdown each time. The very first activity you create for a school year is set as default automatically.

An activity can't be deleted while it already has expense records — delete those expenses first.

12Expense Entry

Where you log class operational spending.

  1. The Activity dropdown is pre-filled with your default (see Activities) — change it if this expense belongs to a different activity.
  2. Enter the date, amount, and a short description (e.g. "Oasis gallon refill + bank admin fee").
  3. Click Save Expense.

A reference number (e.g. CD000007) is generated automatically, matching the style used by formal accounting exports. The Expense History table below lets you search, edit, or delete past entries.

13Expense Ledger

A detailed "general ledger" style report (Buku Besar), grouping every expense by account with a running balance — the same format used by formal school bookkeeping exports.

  • Each account gets its own section: starting balance, every transaction with a running total, and an ending balance.
  • 🖨 Print Report — prints the full ledger.
  • 🖼️ Save as Image (HD) — downloads a high-resolution PNG of the whole report, handy for sharing digitally or archiving.

14Cash Balance

This is the single running bank account (e.g. Bank Mandiri) that ties everything together — it's the only place in the app that combines money in and money out into one real balance:

Current Balance  =  Opening Balance  +  All Payments  −  All Expenses

Setting the opening balance

Do this once — enter whatever the account balance already was before you started using this app. Every payment and expense recorded afterward (in any school year) is added or subtracted automatically; you never need to touch this number again unless it needs correcting.

Carries over across school years: unlike almost everything else in the app, this balance is not reset per school year — it's one continuous account.

Cash Flow Mutation

A chronological list of every payment (cash in) and expense (cash out) across all school years, with a running balance next to each row — this is your day-by-day bank ledger.

🖨 Print Report and 🖼️ Save as Image (HD) work the same way as the Expense Ledger, formatted as a proper account statement.

15Manage Users

Admin only — add other teachers or treasurers who need access.

  1. Enter their email and a temporary password (at least 6 characters).
  2. Choose their role: Treasurer (can enter and view data) or Admin (can also manage other users).
  3. Click + Add User.
  4. Share the email and temporary password with them privately — they can change the password themselves via "Forgot password" on the login screen.

To remove someone's access, click Revoke Access next to their name. Their login account still exists, but they can no longer read or write this class's data.

16Settings

Everything configuration-related lives here, organized top to bottom:

  • Class Identity — school name, class name, default dues amount, homeroom teacher, and treasurer name for the current school year.
  • Manage School Years — see School Years above.
  • Chart of Accounts and Activities — see sections 10 and 11.
  • Firebase Connection and Firestore security rules.
  • Backup / Restore.

17Firebase Connection

The app comes with a Firebase project built in by default, so most classes never need to touch this section. It's only relevant if you want to connect your own separate Firebase project instead.

  1. Paste your own Firebase config (JSON) into the text box.
  2. Click Connect to This Config. The page will reload and connect to your project.
  3. To go back to the built-in project, click Restore Default Config.
  4. Force Local Mode temporarily disables Firebase on this device only, for testing without logging in. Nothing on the online database is affected.

Security Rules

Whenever a new feature needing a new type of data is added, its Firestore Security Rules need to be published in the Firebase Console, or you'll see "Missing or insufficient permissions" errors. The exact rules text to copy is always shown in this section — copy it, go to Firebase Console → Firestore Database → Rules, paste over everything, and click Publish.

If you still see permission errors after publishing: wait 30–60 seconds for the rules to propagate, then fully close and reopen the browser tab (a hard refresh with Ctrl+Shift+R often works too).

18Backup & Restore

Local mode only — relevant only if you're not connected to Firebase.

  • ⬇ Download Backup (.json) — saves everything (school years, students, payments, accounts, activities, expenses, and the bank balance) to a file.
  • ⬆ Restore from Backup — pick a previously downloaded file to overwrite the current local data with it.
Restoring completely replaces all current local data — there's no undo, so only restore a backup if you're sure.

If you're connected to Firebase, the best way to back up is through the Firebase Console → Firestore → Export feature instead.

19Tips & Troubleshooting

"Missing or insufficient permissions" errors

This almost always means the Firestore Security Rules shown in Settings haven't been published yet (or a new one was added after your last publish). Re-copy and re-publish the full rules text, wait a minute, then hard-refresh.

The sidebar shows "Local mode (not connected)"

Either Firebase couldn't be reached (check your internet connection) or someone clicked Force Local Mode on this device. Check Settings → Firebase Connection for the exact status message.

A print looks empty or has an extra blank page

Try the Print button again after a fresh page load. Each report tab builds its own printable copy right before printing, so if a print looks wrong, reloading the page and trying again usually resolves it.

Numbers don't match what I expect

  • Dashboard stats and the Report Summary matrix are scoped to the currently selected school year only.
  • Cash Balance is the one exception — it always reflects all school years combined, since it's one continuous bank account.

I changed an Activity's linked account

Existing expense history for that activity moves with it — the Expense Ledger groups by account at the time you view the report, not at the time the expense was recorded. Keep this in mind if you reorganize your Chart of Accounts later.
